Digital Design and Computer Organization is a foundational area of computer science that focuses on the design and functioning of digital systems and the structure of computers. The primary aim is to understand how computers process data at the hardware level, from basic circuits to complex architectures. This field encompasses the study of logic gates, flip-flops, multiplexers, and other components that form the building blocks of digital circuits. It also covers how these components are organized to execute tasks efficiently. By learning digital design and computer organization, students gain insight into how software interacts with hardware, enabling them to design better systems, optimize performance, and troubleshoot hardware-related issues. Ultimately, the goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical algorithms and practical, hardware-based execution.
